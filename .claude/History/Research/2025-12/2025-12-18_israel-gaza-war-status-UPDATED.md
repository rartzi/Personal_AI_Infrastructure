## Executive Summary

As of December 18, 2025, the Israel-Gaza conflict has undergone dramatic transformation since the October 10, 2025 ceasefire. All living Israeli hostages have been released, but negotiations have stalled on Phase 2 implementation amid disputes over Hamas disarmament and governance. The cumulative death toll has reached 70,669 Palestinians since October 2023, with 395 additional deaths post-ceasefire. The Trump administration's "20-Point Plan" now governs diplomatic efforts, while the ICC has upheld arrest warrants for Netanyahu and Gallant, prompting US sanctions against ICC judges. Hamas maintains 10,000-20,000 fighters operating in guerrilla mode, and winter storms are compounding the humanitarian crisis despite modest improvements in food access.

## 1. Ceasefire Negotiations Status (December 2025)

### Current State

**Status: STALLED** following October 10, 2025 ceasefire agreement

The conflict entered a new phase when a US-brokered ceasefire took effect on **October 10, 2025**. Phase 1 (hostage/prisoner exchange and initial aid) has been largely executed, but Phase 2 negotiations are now deadlocked.

### Governing Framework: The "Trump 20-Point Plan"

- **Leadership:** President Donald Trump personally chairs the proposed "Board of Peace"
- **Key Components:**
  - Complete disarmament of Hamas
  - Israeli withdrawal from Gaza
  - Establishment of International Security Force (ISF)
  - Technocratic Palestinian interim government (elections targeted for 2027)
  - Reconstruction oversight by Trump-chaired "Board of Peace"

### Current Negotiations (December 2025)

**Location:** Miami summit hosted by US Special Envoy **Steve Witkoff**

**Participants:**
- United States
- Qatar
- Egypt
- Turkey

### Major Sticking Points

**1. Hamas Disarmament**
- **Issue:** Hamas explicitly refuses to disarm
- **Israel's Position:** Core requirement for Phase 2
- **Hamas's Position:** Willing to discuss but maintains it cannot be compelled to give up weapons

**2. Final Hostage**
- **Issue:** Israel demands return of Ran Gvili's remains before proceeding to Phase 2
- **Challenge:** Adverse weather conditions complicating recovery efforts

**3. Governance & Security**
- **Dispute:** Disagreements persist over internationally supervised technocratic governing body for Gaza and deployment of ISF
- **Hamas Position:** Actively working to block any alternative Palestinian governing body that excludes them

**4. Humanitarian Access**
- **Hamas Demand:** Significantly increased international pressure on Israel to open border crossings and allow more aid
- **Context:** Critical food scarcity persists despite ceasefire

### Ceasefire Violations (December 2025)

**IDF Reporting:**
- 12 ceasefire violations by Palestinian militants between December 4-18, 2025

**Hamas Accusations:**
- Israel continuing strikes
- Failing to meet aid quotas

**Confidence Level:** VERY HIGH (official diplomatic sources, current negotiations)

**Healthcare Worker Casualties (as of September 2025):**
- **1,722 healthcare workers killed**

**Sources:** UN OCHA, UNRWA, WFP, humanitarian agencies

### Displacement Crisis

**Current Status:**
- **850,000 people** living in 761 displacement sites
- **793,700+ displacement movements** recorded since October 2025 ceasefire
- **658,700 people** moved from southern to northern Gaza post-ceasefire

**Winter Storm Impact (Mid-December 2025):**
- **Storm Byron** and heavy rainfall putting **795,000 displaced people** at risk of flooding
- December 15-16: Flooding impacted **4,700+ people** across 44 emergency shelters
- Over 90% of recent movements involve families traveling in light trucks due to overcrowding in Khan Younis

### Food Security

**Significant Improvement Since Mid-2025:**

**Consumption:**
- **Current:** Average of **2 meals per day**
- **July 2025:** Only 1 meal per day

**Market Prices:**
- **Current:** 132% higher than pre-conflict levels
- **July 2025:** 3,000% above average
- **Assessment:** Drastic improvement but still severe

**Aid Distribution:**
- WFP reached over 1 million people with food boxes and wheat flour
- **1.57 million meals** delivered daily through community kitchens
- Preventive nutrition programs reaching over 300,000 people

### Healthcare System Status

**Infrastructure Devastation:**
- **94% of hospitals damaged or destroyed**
- 51 Health Service Points operational (mid-December)
- New field hospital by International Medical Corps added 200 beds

**Critical Shortages:**
- **52% of critical medications** completely out of stock
- Near-total absence of orthopedic external fixators
- No supplies for open-heart surgery

**Maternal Health Crisis:**
- Maternal deaths and miscarriages **tripled** compared to pre-war levels

**Amputation Crisis:**
- **6,000+ people** require amputation care
- **3,000-4,000 children** underwent at least one limb amputation

### Aid Access Challenges

**Logistics:**
- Aid delivery frequently hampered by prioritization of commercial goods over humanitarian cargo
- UNRWA reports severe blockade on supply pipeline - winter essentials waiting months to enter

**NGO Registration Crisis:**
- New vague registration system threatens to deregister dozens of international NGOs by end of December 2025
- Could force closure of **one-third of remaining health facilities**

**Access Denials:**
- **December 15, 2025:** Only 4 out of 9 coordinated humanitarian movements facilitated by Israeli authorities
- WHO mission to collect medical supplies denied access

## 6. Hamas Status (December 2025)

### Leadership Structure

Following successive eliminations of top leadership, Hamas operates with disrupted command structure.

**Top Political Leadership:**
- **Khaled Meshaal:** Resumed leadership of broader political organization from abroad
- Following assassinations of:
  - **Ismail Haniyeh** (July 2024)
  - **Yahya Sinwar** (October 2024)
  - **Mohammed Sinwar** (Yahya's brother, killed May 2025)

**Gaza Military Command:**
- Situation opaque due to communication breakdowns
- Remaining operational command believed held by:
  - **Izz al-Din al-Haddad**
  - **Raed Saad**
- **Critical Change:** Local cells operate with high autonomy

### Military Capabilities

**Fighter Strength (October 2025 Intelligence Estimates):**
- **10,000-20,000 armed operatives**
- **Increase from December 2024:** Previously 9,000-12,000 fighters
- Bolstered by ongoing recruitment of local youth within Gaza despite significant combat losses

**Represents ~40-80% of original pre-war fighting force** (down from ~25,000+ in October 2023)

**Infrastructure:**
- **Tunnel Network:** Extensive network remains critical strategic asset
- Significant portion still intact despite IDF operations
- Used for: Concealment, logistics, launching surprise attacks
- Proving difficult for Israeli forces to fully neutralize

**Weaponry:**
- **Shift to guerrilla armaments:**
  - Improvised explosive devices (IEDs)
  - Mines
  - Scavenged unexploded ordnance
- **Reduction in rocket capabilities:** Large-scale barrages diminished in frequency and volume

### Military Strategy (December 2025)

**Strategic Shift:** From governance to survival and attrition

**Primary Approach:**
1. **Survival & Attrition**
   - Core tenet: Survive Israeli offensive
   - Belief: Prolonged conflict will exhaust Israeli military resolve
   - Goal: Increase international diplomatic pressure on Israel

2. **Guerrilla Tactics**
   - "Hit-and-run" ambushes
   - Sniper attacks
   - Booby-trapped infrastructure
   - Inflicting steady casualties without direct confrontation

3. **Political Leverage**
   - **Hostages:** Successfully leveraged for October 2025 ceasefire and prisoner exchange
   - **Disarmament Refusal:** Using as negotiating chip
   - **Humanitarian Crisis:** Using dire Gaza situation to demand ceasefire continuation

4. **Political Maneuvering**
   - **Blocking Alternative Governance:** Actively working to prevent any Palestinian governing body in Gaza that excludes them
   - **Goal:** Force post-war reality where Hamas remains necessary political actor

**Current Position in Negotiations:**
- Refuses complete disarmament
- Willing to discuss terms but maintains cannot be compelled to give up weapons
- Using Gvili remains as leverage

**Sources:** IDF Intelligence, US Intelligence Community (ODNI), Council on Foreign Relations, ACLED

**Confidence Level:** HIGH (multiple intelligence assessments, observable tactical shifts)
